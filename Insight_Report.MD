#  Medical Insurance Cost Analysis – Insights Report

##  1. Smoking is the biggest cost driver
- Average charges for smokers: **32,050**
- Average charges for non-smokers: **8,434**
➡ Smokers pay **4× more**.  
This is the strongest predictor of insurance charges.

---

##  2. Age strongly impacts medical cost
- Age 18–25: lowest cost  
- After age 40: charges rise sharply  
- Age 50–60: highest cost group  
➡ Risk increases non-linearly.

---

##  3. BMI category impact
| BMI Category | Avg Charges |
|--------------|-------------|
| Underweight  | 8,852       |
| Normal       | 10,379      |
| Overweight   | 10,993      |
| **Obese**    | **15,479**  |

➡ Obese people pay **50% more** than normal weight.

---

##  4. Children increase insurance cost
More dependents = more medical usage.

---

##  5. Male vs Female Charges
- Male average: 13,956  
- Female average: 12,569  

➡ Gender alone small impact, but gender + smoking huge impact.

---

##  6. High-risk customer profile
People with:
✔ High BMI  
✔ High Age  
✔ Smoker status  

➡ These categories combined lead to maximum charges.

---

##  7. Highest & Lowest Charge Profiles

### Highest Charges:
- Age 54  
- Female  
- Smoker  
- BMI 47.41  
- Charges: **63,770**

### Lowest Charges:
- Age 18  
- Male  
- BMI 23  
- Non-smoker  
- Charges: **1121**

➡ Perfect contrast between high-risk & low-risk customers.

---

##  Conclusion
Insurance cost is significantly influenced by lifestyle choices such as smoking and obesity, combined with age. These findings help insurance companies design fair premium pricing models.
